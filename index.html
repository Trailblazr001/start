For Whom

This course is designed for:

  • Software developers willing to become AI Practitioners
  • Data Engineers willing to utilize their data pipelines for new AI applications
  • Everyone who feels like there's a lot of buzz in the AI space but isn't seeing the underlying technology

What

Our 8-week course is designed to take you on a journey from the basics of LLMs to building your own model. Here's a breakdown of what you can expect each week:

  • Week 1: "Pipeline Primer" - Setting Up Your Data Loading Pipeline Learn how to implement a data loading pipeline in AIML and review relevant code in PyTorch.
  • Week 2: "Tokenization Tales" - Training a BPE Tokenizer from Scratch Watch video tutorials on training a BPE tokenizer and review the chapter on attention mechanisms.
  • Week 3: "Model Mastery" - Implementing the Model Architecture in AIML Read the chapters on implementing the model architecture in AIML and start building your LLM.
  • Week 4: "Pretraining Palooza" - Pretraining the LLM Watch tutorial videos on pretraining the LLM and read the chapter on pretraining and loading pretrained weights.
  • Week 5: "Fine-Tuning Frenzy" - Fine-Tuning the LLM and Adding Bells and Whistles Read the chapters on fine-tuning the LLM and review the code for instruction fine-tuning.
  • Week 6: "Efficient Fine-Tuning" - Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning with LoRA Read the appendix on parameter-efficient fine-tuning with LoRA and learn how to optimize your LLM.
  • Week 7: "Coming back to the Roots with C Coding Challenge" - Coding the LLM in C Check out coding repositories available online and learn how to code the LLM in C.
  • Final Week: "Build and Share" - Building Something Cool and Sharing it with the World Use your newfound skills to build something cool and share it with the world!

How

Our 8-week course is built on top of the following resources:

  • "LLMs from Scratch" by Sebastian Raschka
  • Materials from Andrej Karpathy
  • "Deep Learning" by Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, and Aaron Courville
You can choose from two options to complete the course:

Option 1: Self-Study

  • Goal setting and progress check-in in the middle of the course
  • Review of the next steps as a touchpoint on the project to build at the end of the course
  • Optional weekly reminders on the week's content

Option 2: With Community

In addition to all the self-study materials, you'll also get:

  • Small class size for a closer learning experience
  • Online chat with participants for Q&A and aligning on understanding
  • Community study with boosted engagement and motivation
  • Bi-weekly sessions on practical topics such as RAG, Agents, data security, Responsible AI, instruction fine-tuning, and application system design
  • Interactive quizzes to ensure comprehension and assess progress (not just for grades, but to ensure understanding)
